§ 01 — The Problem

What's Breaking Today

Title 10 AGR position management has no authoritative system of record. IPPS-A is incomplete for T10, assignments are frequently wrong, and large portions of the population — students, command list positions, medical holds — are invisible to the manning picture.

Issue 01
Dual Slotting

Legacy systems created duplicate position records to represent transitions. The same person appears in two slots with no relationship defined — generating false data that masks both valid handoffs and genuine problems.

Issue 02
IPPS-A Assignment Errors

PeopleSoft's PS_JOB table was designed for commercial HR. It has no concept of position succession — only concurrent assignments. The ARNG workaround annex documents multi-step hacks required just to process an AGR retirement correctly.

Issue 03
No Voucher Visibility

OPRs have a ceiling of authorized vouchers from NGB, but there is no tool that shows which positions are actually funded (voucher applied) vs. positions where a soldier is assigned with no authorization behind it.

Issue 04
Invisible Population Segments

Students at PME, centrally-selected command leaders on the CLP (Command List Position) awaiting assignment, medical holds in the MEB/PEB pipeline, and PPP excess soldiers all consume end-strength authorizations but don't appear in position-based manning reports — creating a gap between authorized, assigned, and present-for-duty strength.

§ 02 — The Authorization Chain

Federal Position Lifecycle

Every Title 10 AGR position flows through a federal authorization chain before a soldier can legitimately fill it. This chain is the data backbone of the model.

01
HQDA Ceiling

Congress authorizes end-strength by grade and skill. NGB establishes the annual ceiling within those limits.

02
NGB-ARZ-T Approval

Every T10 AGR position must be individually approved by the NGB Staff Management Office before it can be included on a TDA.

03
TDA Authorization

The approved position is documented on a Table of Distribution and Allowances with a specific paragraph, line, and UIC. This is the voucher.

04
OPR Applies Voucher

The OPR allocates from their authorized pool. Applying the voucher to a position makes it funded. No voucher means no authorized fill.

05
Slating Board

NGB runs OML-based slating. Selected soldiers receive tentative assignment. CNGB publishes federal orders — not state-issued.

06
Assignment Record

Funded position + person + published orders create the assignment record. This triggers the outgoing/incoming cycle.

§ 03 — The Billet Cycle

Outgoing / Incoming Model

Every funded billet carries two slots simultaneously during a transition period. The outgoing holder has an end date. The incoming backfill has a start date. This relationship — not a duplicate position — is the correct data structure.

§ 04 — Full Population Inventory

Where Every T10 AGR Soldier Lives

The T10 AGR population extends far beyond soldiers filling positions. Students, command list positions (CLP), medical holds, transients, and excess soldiers all consume end-strength authorizations. Every segment must be tracked to reconcile authorized vs. assigned vs. present-for-duty strength.

■
Authorized & Assigned

Soldiers physically filling a funded TDA position and performing duty. This is the core population — position is filled, voucher is applied, soldier is present.

Fills Position
▶
Students (TTHS-S)

Soldiers at PME (SLC, ALC, SMA, ILE, AWC), MOSQ courses, functional courses, or civilian education under AGR. Position is held on paper but soldier is physically absent. Position cannot be backfilled. Tracked via ATRRS. Courses over 139 days fall into formal Student category.

Consumes End-Strength Position Gap
★
CLP — Command List Position

Centrally selected command leaders — CSMs, battalion/brigade commanders — managed by NGB Human Capital Management (HCM). Selected through HQDA CSM Selection Boards or Centralized Selection List (CSL) for officers. Carried on the CLP/COP (Command Order of Precedence) and tracked centrally by NGB, not against a specific state TDA line. Consumes T10 end-strength but has no position authorization until slated to a vacancy. Governed by PPOM 24-013, CNGBI 0160.00 (Senior Enlisted Bench), and NGR 600-5.

Consumes End-Strength No TDA Authorization
⇄
Transients (TTHS-T)

Soldiers PCS between assignments — permissive TDY, house-hunting, travel. Position at losing unit is vacant; gaining unit position not yet occupied. Creates a temporary gap in both units' fill rates.

Position Gap
⚗
Medical Hold / DES Pipeline

Soldiers not medically fit for duty, referred into the Disability Evaluation System. MEB (~76 days) followed by PEB (~102 days). Retained on AGR status per AR 635-40 throughout. Occupies end-strength but may not be performing duties. May result in TDRL or medical retirement.

Consumes End-Strength Position Gap
↺
PPP / Excess / Over-Grade

Soldiers displaced by reorganization, force structure changes, or ceiling reductions. Managed through Priority Placement Program — gets priority for vacant positions. Carried in excess for up to one year. Released from AGR if not placed.

Consumes End-Strength
⚓
Sanctuary (18–20 Yr)

Soldiers with 18+ years of active federal service but less than 20. Federal law prevents involuntary separation — they must be retained to retirement eligibility. May be in excess or over-grade status. TAG determines disposition.

Consumes End-Strength
⏲
OTOT (One-Time Occasional Tour)

AGR soldiers on a defined-length tour, not career status. Total service may not exceed 5 years. Discharged at end of current order. Distinct from career AGR — does not go through continuation boards.

Fills Position
✈
TDY / CLASP

Soldiers on temporary duty away from home station, or on Career Lateral Assignment Program (broadening assignments). Still assigned to and technically filling their position. Counted against end-strength at home station.

Fills Position
⚠
ASO Holdovers

Soldiers with an Active Service Obligation from military-funded schooling. Retained in AGR status until ASO completion, not beyond MRD/RCP. May be in a position or in excess depending on timing of ASO fulfillment.

Consumes End-Strength
§ 05 — Statutory Authority

Title 10 USC Codes for AGR

The T10 AGR program derives its legal authority from a specific set of United States Code sections. Leadership must understand the distinction between the core AGR authority and the mobilization authorities that may also affect AGR soldiers.

USC Section Title Application to T10 AGR
Primary AGR Authority
10 USC §12310 Reserves: for organizing, administering, etc., reserve components The core AGR statute. Authorizes the Secretary to order reserve members to active duty under 12301(d) to perform AGR duty: organizing, administering, recruiting, instructing, or training the reserve components. Member serves in reserve grade and remains eligible for promotion.
10 USC §12301(d) Reserve components generally (voluntary) The underlying activation authority for AGR. Authorizes voluntary order to active duty with member's consent. All T10 AGR orders are issued under this section. Distinct from T32 AGR which serves under 32 USC §502(f).
10 USC §101(d)(6) Definition — Active Guard and Reserve Defines AGR as a reserve member on active duty under §12301(d) or, for ARNG, on full-time National Guard duty under 32 USC §502(f), performing duty for organizing, administering, recruiting, instructing, or training.
Mobilization / Involuntary Authorities
10 USC §12301(a) Full Mobilization Congress declares war or national emergency. All reserve members can be ordered to AD involuntarily for the duration plus 6 months.
10 USC §12302 Ready Reserve — Partial Mobilization Presidential declaration of national emergency. Up to 1,000,000 Ready Reserve members for up to 24 consecutive months.
10 USC §12304 Presidential Reserve Call-Up (Selected Reserve) No national emergency required. President can activate up to 200,000 Selected Reserve (plus 30,000 IRR) for up to 365 days for operational missions.
10 USC §12304a Reserve call-up for domestic emergencies Governor requests federal reserve support for domestic emergency operations.
10 USC §12304b Preplanned missions in support of combatant commands Secretary of Defense authority for Selected Reserve to support preplanned overseas operations for combatant commanders.
10 USC §12305 Stop-Loss Authority President may suspend laws relating to promotion, retirement, and separation during periods members are serving under §12301, §12302, or §12304. This is the statutory basis for stop-loss.
§ 06 — Tour Length Rules

Tour Management & Service Limits

T10 AGR tour lengths are governed by AR 135-18 and NGR 600-5. Initial tours serve as probationary periods; career continuation requires board approval. Officers are managed to MRD; enlisted to RCP.

Initial Tour
3 Yr

Probationary period for both officers and enlisted. Evaluated during third year for career continuation by chain of command and Initial Tour Continuation Board (ITCB).

Follow-On Tours
6 Yr

Maximum per tour increment after career continuation approval. NGB conducts qualitative and quantitative continuation boards for both officers and enlisted.

OTOT Max
5 Yr

One-Time Occasional Tour soldiers — non-career AGR. Total service may not exceed 5 years. Discharged at end of current order with no continuation board.

Officer Limit
MRD

Mandatory Removal Date based on years of commissioned service and grade. Governed by 10 USC Chapter 1407 provisions. Sanctuary applies at 18+ years.

Enlisted Limit
RCP

Retention Control Points per DA PAM 601-280. Maximum years of active service by grade (e.g., E-7 ~24 yr, E-8 ~26 yr). Sanctuary protection at 18–20 years.

Retirement
20 Yr

Retirement eligibility at 20 years of active federal service under 10 USC Chapter 1223. Soldiers between 18–20 years have sanctuary protection — cannot be involuntarily separated.

§ 07 — Reporting Flags

Anomaly Detection

Six automated flags surface position management problems to HQ staff before they become vacancies, compliance issues, or pay anomalies.

Immediate
No TDA Authorization

A soldier is assigned to a position with no NGB-approved TDA backing it. The position does not legally exist under federal authority.

Detection: position has no linked voucher or TDA para/line in record
Immediate
Grade/Skill Ceiling Breach

The total count of T10 AGR soldiers at a specific grade and skill exceeds the NGB-authorized ceiling for that category.

Detection: aggregate count by grade + MOS vs. authorized ceiling
Immediate
Hard Dual Slot

Same person appears in two positions with the same slot role — both Outgoing or both Incoming — with no valid relationship between them.

Detection: EMPLID in 2+ active assignments; roles match or no relationship defined
Warning
Transition Gap Over 30 Days

The outgoing soldier's end date and the incoming soldier's start date are misaligned by more than 30 days, creating a coverage gap or unauthorized double-fill.

ABS(outgoing_end - incoming_start) > 30 days
Warning
No Incoming Assigned

Outgoing soldier has an end date on record but no incoming backfill has been slated. Position will go vacant at departure.

Detection: outgoing end_date is set; incoming slot is null or empty
Warning
Incoming on Hold

A soldier is slated as incoming but blocked by a clearance adjudication, school seat, or medical hold. The position appears filled but the backfill is operationally soft.

Detection: incoming slot filled; hold_reason is not null
§ 08 — Governing Regulations

Policy References for Leadership

The regulatory framework governing T10 AGR position management spans DoD instructions, Army regulations, and NGB-specific guidance.

NGR 600-5
The Active Guard Reserve (AGR) Program — FTNGD Management (ARNG)

Master regulation for ARNG AGR management: selection, assignment, utilization, tour management, continuation boards, PPP, separation, and retirement. Updated 12 May 2023.

AR 135-18
The Active Guard Reserve (AGR) Program

DA-level policy for AGR across ARNG and USAR: eligibility, tour lengths, career management, medical fitness, MRD/RCP rules.

DoDI 1205.18
Full-Time Support (FTS) to the Reserve Components

DoD-level policy establishing AGR as a career program. Governs lifecycle management and end-strength accounting across all services.

DoDI 1215.06
Uniform Reserve, Training, and Retirement Categories

Defines reserve categories including how AGR fits within the Selected Reserve for accounting and management purposes.

CNGBI 0160.00
Senior Enlisted Leadership Bench Program

Governs management of T10 E9 positions reporting to general officers. Establishes the Senior Enlisted Bench and CSELAB advisory board for command leadership assignment.

AR 635-40
Physical Evaluation for Retention, Retirement, or Separation

Governs the MEB/PEB disability evaluation process for AGR soldiers. Determines medical retention, TDRL, or medical retirement.

DA PAM 601-280
Army Retention Program

Contains the Retention Control Point (RCP) tables that establish maximum years of active service by enlisted grade.

NGR 600-100 / 600-200
Commissioned Officers / Enlisted Personnel Management

ARNG-specific officer and enlisted management regulations that intersect with AGR career management and promotion.

§ 09 — The IPPS-A Problem

Why PS_JOB Breaks for AGR

IPPS-A is built on PeopleSoft HCM — a commercial HR system designed around one core assumption that doesn't hold for military assignments.

-- PS_JOB: The heart of IPPS-A assignment data
-- Primary key: EMPLID + EMPL_RCD + EFFDT + EFFSEQ

SELECT j.emplid, j.empl_rcd, j.effdt, j.effseq, j.action, j.position_nbr
FROM   ps_job j
WHERE  j.effdt = (SELECT MAX(j1.effdt) FROM ps_job j1
              WHERE j1.emplid = j.emplid
              AND   j1.empl_rcd = j.empl_rcd
              AND   j1.effdt <= SYSDATE)
AND    j.effseq = (SELECT MAX(j2.effseq) FROM ps_job j2
                WHERE j2.emplid = j.emplid
                AND   j2.empl_rcd = j.empl_rcd
                AND   j2.effdt = j.effdt)

-- This correlated subquery just to find "current assignment."
-- Add EMPL_RCD > 0 for concurrent records, and it fails without
-- joining PS_PER_ORG_INST to identify the substantive record.

-- No position_succession table exists.
-- No outgoing/incoming relationship native to the schema.
-- Projected end date manually maintained — frequently stale.
-- No TTHS category tracking (students, transients, holdees).
-- CLP (Command List Position) soldiers not tracked in schema.

What IPPS-A Gets Wrong

PeopleSoft's person model assumes one primary employment relationship. "Additional assignments" (EMPL_RCD > 0) are designed for concurrent roles at the same organization — not for modeling a position handoff between two different soldiers. The official ARNG workaround annex documents a multi-step hack to process an AGR retirement that deliberately avoids hitting the soldier's iPERMS record. There is no TTHS categorization, no CLP (Command List Position) tracking, and no way to distinguish a soldier filling a position from one centrally managed by NGB awaiting command assignment.

What Your System Owns

IPPS-A data is an input signal — useful for EMPLID, grade, MOS, and orders numbers. It is not a source of truth for who is actually in what position. Your system builds and owns the position-to-person relationship table with explicit outgoing/incoming modeling, funded/unfunded state, the full federal authorization chain, and a complete population inventory covering all 10 segments — from position-holders to students, CLP command leaders, medical holds, PPP excess, and sanctuary soldiers.
