# REDM — Rapid Engagement Delivery Model

> **Created by James Crandall** — Power Platform Solution Architect, Army National Guard

REDM is an Agile-Plus delivery framework engineered for Microsoft Power Platform and high-tempo operational environments. It delivers working capability faster than traditional Agile, reduces risk through continuous stakeholder presence, and builds governance in from day one.

## The Model in One Sentence

REDM is Agile recalibrated for a world where a single developer can build an enterprise system in a week — and traditional ceremonies are the only thing slowing it down.

---

## Core Tenets

| # | Tenet | What It Means |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Mission First | Every decision is evaluated against operational need, not process compliance |
| 2 | Build to Learn | Requirements emerge through usage — not documentation |
| 3 | Continuous Presence | Architect and stakeholders stay engaged throughout; no handoff points |
| 4 | Rapid Progress | Working capability is the only progress metric that matters |
| 5 | Governance Built In | Security, schema ownership, and ALM from day one |
| 6 | Iterate Relentlessly | Low-code makes change cheap — REDM exploits that |
| 7 | Platform-Led Design | The right Power Platform tool for the right job |
| 8 | IOC Over Perfection | Get capability in real users' hands fast; perfection follows real data |

---

## The Six Phases

```
Phase 1 — Mission Alignment     (1–3 days)
Phase 2 — Co-Design             (3–7 days)
Phase 3 — Rapid Build Cycles    (continuous; 1–2 week cadence)
Phase 4 — Validation & Hardening (1–2 weeks)
Phase 5 — Deployment & Training  (1 week)
Phase 6 — Sustainment & Growth   (ongoing)
```
